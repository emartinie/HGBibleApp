Who Was Paul?

Paul was an apostle, teacher, traveler, and letter writer whose encounter with Jesus redirected the whole course of his life.

A life between worlds

Born in Tarsus and educated in Jerusalem, Paul moved between Jewish tradition and the wider Roman world. Those overlapping identities shaped both his ministry and the language of his letters.

Before becoming a witness to Jesus, Paul opposed the early believers. Acts presents his calling not as a small adjustment, but as a reversal: the persecutor becomes a servant of the message he once tried to silence.

Called and sent

Paul understood his apostleship as a calling received rather than a status achieved. His journeys carried the gospel into cities connected by trade, migration, and Roman roads.

His letters were written into real tensions: divided churches, difficult questions, suffering communities, and believers learning how the good news reshaped ordinary life.

Reading Paul well

Paul is easiest to hear when we remember that his letters belong to conversations already in progress. Each passage has a local setting, yet continually points beyond itself toward Christ, the church, and faithful life.
