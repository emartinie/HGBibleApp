🕍 Hillel Calendar (Rabbinic) The Hillel calendar is the standard Jewish calendar used today. It is: • lunar-based (months follow moon cycles) • mathematically calculated, not observational • includes leap months to stay aligned with seasons It was formalized to ensure: • unity across dispersed Jewish communities • predictable feast days year after year Key idea: A reliable, shared system for all Israel—regardless of location. ⸻ 🌿 Enochian Calendar (364-Day Solar Model) The Enochian calendar is based on a 364-day solar cycle, found in texts like 1 Enoch and some Dead Sea Scroll traditions. It features: • exactly 52 weeks (perfect weekly alignment) • feast days always fall on the same day of the week each year • no lunar dependency Key idea: A perfectly ordered, unchanging calendar aligned to a structured creation pattern. ⸻ 🕊️ Zedokian Calendar (Priestly Restoration Model) The Zedokian calendar attempts to restore the priestly calendar associated with the sons of Zadok. It typically emphasizes: • Biblical authority over later rabbinic tradition • observational or reconstructed timing • alignment with temple service cycles There is no single agreed version, but all share the goal of: Key idea: Returning to what is believed to be the original priestly timing of appointed times.
